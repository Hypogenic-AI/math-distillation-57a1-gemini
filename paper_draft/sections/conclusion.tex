\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

In this paper, we have presented a systematic distillation of the 22-million-edge implication graph from the Equational Theories Project into a compact 9.1KB format. By leveraging structural analysis, syntactic heuristics, and algebraic duality, we have demonstrated that it is possible to compress a massive repository of mathematical knowledge into a format suitable for use in resource-constrained environments like LLMs.

Our results show that the implication graph of 4,694 magma laws contains significant redundancy, which can be effectively managed through strongly connected components and semantic clustering. We have also identified key syntactic rules that accurately predict many non-implications and exceptions where these rules fail.

The resulting distillation, which covers over 50\% of the laws in the ETP dataset, provides a robust framework for enhancing the mathematical reasoning capabilities of small-scale models. Future research should focus on more sophisticated compression techniques, the integration of latent space representations, and the validation of our distillation against a wider range of mathematical problems and reasoning tasks.
